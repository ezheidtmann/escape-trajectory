6 /**
7  * Implements the restricted three-body problem.
8  *
9  * In this problem, there are two bodies of comparable mass orbiting
10  * their common center of mass. Due to carefully chosen initial conditions,
11  * we can assume that they orbit the center of mass in concentric
12  * circles. We call these two bodies Earth and Sun, but the mass ratio
13  * is not normally as extreme as in the actual solar system.
14  *
15  * We then launch a projectile from Earth within the plane of the massive
16  * bodies and calculate its trajectory. The equations of motion of the
17  * projectile are:
18  *
19  * \f{eqnarray*}
20  * \ddot{x} & = & -\frac{GM_E}{d_E^3}\left(x-L_E\cos\omega t\right)-
21  * \frac{GM_S}{d_S^3}\left(x+L_S\cos\omega t\right)\\
22  * \ddot{y} & = & -\frac{GM_E}{d_E^3}\left(y-L_E\sin\omega t\right)-
23  * \frac{GM_S}{d_S^3}\left(y+L_S\sin\omega t\right)
24  * \f}
25  *
26  * where \f$M_E\f$ is the mass of the earth, \f$M_S\f$ is the mass of
27  * the sun, \f$L_E\f$ and \f$L_S\f$ are the distances from the center of
28  * mass to the earth and sun respectively, and \f$d_E\f$ and \f$d_S\f$
29  * are the distances from the projectile to the earth and sun
30  * respectively.
31  */
