37 /**
38  * Add a step of motion. If the projectile moves from the point
39  * \f$\overrightarrow{r_0} = \langle x_0, y_0 \rangle\f$ to
40  * \f$\overrightarrow{r} = \langle x, y \rangle\f$, then
41  * the magnitude of the angle between the vectors is found by
42  *
43  * \f{eqnarray*}
44  * \left|\Theta\right| &=& \arccos \left( \hat{r_0} \cdot \hat{r} \right)\\
45  * &=& \arccos \left( \frac{xx_0 + yy_0}
46  * {\sqrt{\left(x^2+y^2\right)\left(x_0^2+y_0^2\right)}} \right)
47  * \f}
48  *
49  * The direction of rotation is given by the direction of the cross product
50  * of the vectors. Because both \f$\overrightarrow{r_0}\f$ and
51  * \f$\overrightarrow{r}\f$ are 2-dimensional, the cross product is
52  * zero in all components except the \f$z\f$ component, which has value
53  * \f$x_0y - xy_0\f$.
54  *
55  * If this quantity is greater than zero, \f$\Theta\f$ is positive
56  * (corresponding to a counter-clockwise rotation). If
57  * the quantity is less than zero, \f$\Theta\f$ is negative (corresponding
58  * to a clockwise rotation).
59  *
60  * If the computed angle is invalid, as might occur near the origin or when
61  * some of the values are very small, we define \f$\Theta \equiv 0\f$.
62  *
63  * When called for the first time, only the previous point is updated.
64  * No computation is performed.
65  *
66  * @param x X-coordinate, with origin at center of rotation
67  * @param y Y-coordinate, with origin at center of rotation
68  */
